What is an LLM?

So far, we have entered our Chai Coffee Shop and placed our order. But who is actually making the Chai?

The answer is the GPT Model, which is an example of an LLM (Large Language Model).

Large

The model has learned from an enormous amount of publicly available text including books, articles, websites, documentation, and many other sources. "Large" also refers to the billions of parameters that help the model understand language patterns.

Language

Humans communicate through language. Whether we write emails, ask questions, generate code, summarize documents, or tell stories, we are using language. An LLM is trained to understand these patterns and generate meaningful responses.

Model

  • ChatGPT is the coffee shop.
  • GPT is the chef.

The chef has already learned thousands of recipes before opening the shop. Likewise, GPT was trained before you started chatting with it.

Behind the scenes, however, a lot more is happening than simply displaying an answer.

Why doesn't ChatGPT copy from the Internet?

Many people think ChatGPT searches Google every time a question is asked. That's not how it works.

Imagine ordering Ginger Chai. Does the chef leave the shop, visit another tea stall, ask for the recipe, and come back? Of course not.

The chef already knows how to prepare it because he learned it during training. Similarly, GPT uses patterns it learned during training to generate a completely new response by predicting one token at a time.

Why Computers Don't Understand Human Language

Humans understand words. Computers understand numbers.

Before an AI model can understand anything, every word must first be converted into numbers. Only then can mathematical calculations begin.

Tokenization

Think of tokenization as writing your Chai order onto small order slips.

'I' 'love' 'Chai' '.'

Each piece is called a token. Sometimes a token is a whole word, part of a word, or punctuation.

Embeddings

Embeddings convert tokens into numerical representations so that similar words are placed close together in a mathematical space.

Tea Coffee Milk are more closely related than Tea Laptop Rocket

Positional Encoding

The order of words matters. Positional Encoding tells the Transformer where every token appears.

Self-Attention

Just as a chef looks at the complete recipe instead of one ingredient, Self-Attention allows every token to understand the context of other tokens.

Transformer

The Transformer is the brain of modern LLMs. It processes all tokens together while understanding their relationships using Self-Attention.

Summary

  • Text becomes tokens.
  • Tokens become numbers through embeddings.
  • Positional Encoding adds word order.
  • The Transformer understands relationships.
  • The model predicts the next token until a complete response is generated.
