We need to get familiar with a few terms (I would call them jargons for some reason) and understand the flow.

Tokenization

Breaking down the input text into units (tokens). A token can be a whole word, part of a word, or even a single character.

Embeddings

Numerical representations of data, such as words, parts of words, or sentences, converted into lists of coordinates within a multi-dimensional space.

Computers understand numbers. At the backend, everything is binary; however, for simplicity, we can think of them as decimal number representations.

Positional Encoding

A technique used in AI models (like Transformers) to give the model a sense of word order. Since these models process all the words in a sentence simultaneously rather than one by one, positional encoding injects numerical signals to tell the AI which word comes first, second, or last.

Self-Attention

Self-attention is the foundational AI mechanism that allows models (like ChatGPT and other LLMs) to understand context. It enables the AI to weigh the importance of different words in a sequence simultaneously, figuring out exactly how each word relates to every other word in the text.

Summary

Our prompts are converted into tokens, processed through embeddings and positional encoding, and then used to generate the most suitable answer to the question or prompt we have given.
